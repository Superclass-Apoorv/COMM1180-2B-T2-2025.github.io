üìù Writing Your Report: Section-by-Section Guide

Section 1: Introduction (100-150 words)

Purpose: Set the stage for your financing request

  • Brief company background and current market position
  • Clear statement of your business expansion idea
  • How this expansion creates value for the company
  • Preview of why this warrants external financing
Example Opening:

"Woolworths Group, Australia's largest retail chain, has successfully dominated the traditional grocery market through its extensive store network and supply chain efficiency. However, to maintain its competitive edge and capture emerging consumer preferences for sustainable products, the company should expand into the rapidly growing eco-conscious consumer market. This report proposes launching a new line of eco-friendly reusable water bottles, which aligns with Woolworths' commitment to sustainability while opening new revenue streams..."

Section 2: Proposal of Projects (250-300 words)

Purpose: Detail your two project options and their connection to Assessment 2A

Project 1 Structure:
  • Overview & scope
  • Target market
  • Investment requirements
  • Risk profile
  • Marketing, Technology & Accounting Link
Project 2 Structure:
  • Overview & scope
  • Target market
  • Investment requirements
  • Risk profile
  • Marketing, Technology & Accounting Link
Key Success Factor: Clearly explain how each project differs in cost, risk, timeline, and target market. Then show strong connections to your Assessment 2A marketing, technology, and pricing strategies.

Section 3: Investment Analysis (300-400 words)

Purpose: Demonstrate understanding of financial evaluation methods

NPV (Net Present Value)

Best for: Projects with clear cash flow projections

Use when: You can predict revenue streams over time

IRR (Internal Rate of Return)

Best for: Comparing projects with different scales

Use when: You need to compare percentage returns

Payback Period

Best for: High-risk projects needing quick returns

Use when: Speed of capital recovery is crucial

Profitability Index

Best for: When capital is limited

Use when: Comparing return per dollar invested

Remember: You don't need to perform calculations! Focus on explaining why each tool suits your specific project characteristics.

Section 4: Evaluation & Recommendation (300-350 words)

Purpose: Recommend one project (out of the two) with strong justification

Clear Choice

State your recommended project

Stakeholder Value

Show who benefits and how

Strategic Fit

Connect to company mission

Financial Future

Long-term sustainability

Section 5: Conclusion (100-200 words)

Purpose: Summarize and reinforce financing justification

Main findings summary
Restatement of recommendation
Final financing argument
Forward-looking growth statement
